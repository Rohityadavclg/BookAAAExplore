If you are visiting Kedarnath, there are several things you should keep in mind to ensure a safe visit.
  • Kedarnath is located in the Himalayan region, and the weather can be unpredictable.
  • The trek to Kedarnath involves a steep uphill climb. Ensure that you are in good health and have a moderate level of fitness before embarking on the journey.
  • Kedarnath is not a tour; it’s a Yatra, and for that, you’ll have to climb almost 30 km one way, which will take approximately 10 to 12 hours.
  • Once you reach the temple premises, you may need to join a queue to enter the sanctum and have darshan. The waiting time can range from a few minutes to several hours, depending on the crowd size and the time of your visit. During peak pilgrimage seasons, the waiting time can be 4 to 5 hours.
  • You have to apply for a yatra pass on your own. You can visit the official website of the Kedarnath Temple or our team will guide you.
  • The printed yatra pass serves as proof of your registration and eligibility for darshan. The authorities may require you to show the printed pass for verification purposes at different checkpoints along the trekking route or at the temple entrance.
  • Carry your own yatra pass print with you to obtain a token. You will typically need to present your printed yatra pass and follow the instructions provided by the authorities.
  • We don’t provide specific services or facilitate travel arrangements to reach the temple. To reach the Kedarnath Temple, you can consider the following options on your own:
  • Helicopter Services: Helicopter services are available from various locations to reach Kedarnath, such as Phata, Guptkashi, or Sersi. You can check heliyatra.itrctc.co.in for availability, schedules, and bookings.
  • Mules or Palanquins: Along the trekking route, you may find options to hire mules or palanquins (doli) to assist you in reaching the temple. These can be helpful if you have difficulty walking or prefer not to trek the entire distance.
  • For Abhishek (ritual bath) or Pooja (worship ceremony) at Kedarnath Temple, you have to make the reservation on your own. This may involve filling out a form, selecting the type of abhishek or pooja, providing the date and time preference, and making the payment at the counter which is near the temple.
  • During peak pilgrimage seasons, sometimes you may have to walk down from Sonprayag to Sitapur (2/3 km) due to traffic and parking issues.
  • In accordance with religious sentiments and practices, only vegetarian food is typically available and served during the yatra. This is to maintain the sanctity of the pilgrimage and to honor the beliefs of the devotees.
