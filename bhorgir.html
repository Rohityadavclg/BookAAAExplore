About Bhimashankar Bhorgiri Trek

The Bhorgiri Bhimashankar Trek is a popular trekking route located in the Sahyadri mountain range of Maharashtra, India. It offers a thrilling and scenic trekking experience, taking you through dense forests, rugged terrains, and picturesque landscapes.

The trek starts from the village of Bhorgiri, which is approximately 70 kilometers away from Pune. From Bhorgiri, the trail winds its way through lush green forests, offering glimpses of diverse flora and fauna along the way. The monsoon season, from June to September, is an ideal time to undertake this trek as the entire region transforms into a verdant paradise with numerous waterfalls and streams.

The trek to Bhimashankar, a famous pilgrim site dedicated to Lord Shiva, is moderate in difficulty and can be completed within a day. The total trek distance is around 6-7 kilometers, with an elevation gain of approximately 1,200 meters.

As you ascend, you will encounter steep slopes, rocky patches, and mesmerizing viewpoints offering breathtaking vistas of the surrounding valleys. Keep an eye out for wildlife such as langurs, birds, and if you're lucky, you may even spot some rare species like the Malabar Giant Squirrel.

The final stretch of the trek takes you to Bhimashankar Temple, an ancient and revered shrine of Lord Shiva. Explore the temple complex, witness the religious rituals, and soak in the spiritual ambiance before beginning the descent back to Bhorgiri.

The Bhorgiri Bhimashankar Trek is a fantastic opportunity to connect with nature, challenge yourself, and experience the beauty of the Sahyadri Mountains. It attracts both adventure enthusiasts and pilgrims seeking a blend of adventure and spirituality.

Things to Carry
    • Luggage Packed in Big Good Sack, All Luggage Wrap Properly.
    • Good Trekking Shoes.
    • Extra pair of clothes and socks.
    • Dry Snacks.
    • Electral/Glucon-D energizer drinks.
    • 2-liter water bottle. (1 liter each)
    • A Sweater/ Warm wear/ Rainwear. Wear comfortable clothing during trek (full sleeves Shirt T-Shirt, Avoid Jeans).
    • Head Torch / Hand Torch Compulsory.
    • Cap / Hat, goggles, sunscreen, Odomos.
    • Camera, Binocular (Optional).
    • Identity proof (must).
    • Toiletries (Napkin, Face Wash, Tissue, Toilet paper, Sanitizer).
    • Slippers (At the end of day or trek so you feel relax).
    • Personal Medicine if any.
